Events | Come out and be merry with us.

ARCC puts on a variety of events for you to learn about the Chinese Culture. This page gives you just a brief glimpse into a few of them. Take a look around, then come on out to enjoy delicious food, meet new people, and watch breath-taking performances. More events will be added to this page as they come up.


Note that all of the following gorgeous photos are courtesy of David Hsu. You can check out his website here.

  • Chinese New Year Dinner

    Sun January 31, 2016

    UC Peter/Wright/McKenna

    The Lunar New Year is the biggest holiday celebrated by East Asians. Originally, the purpose of the celebration was to welcome the coming of Spring in the farming communities. ARCC celebrates the Chinese New Year by hosting an annual dinner with authentic Chinese food.

  • Night Market

    Spring 2016

    UC Wean Commons

    In Mainland China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and other Asian countries, nightlife is something truly unique. When the sun sets, independent vendors with carts fill the streets with delicious food, clothing, jewelry and anything you may wish to buy. The streets are alive with laughter, an appetizing aroma, and crowds of happy people everywhere. ARCC seeks to recreate the lively night atmosphere with its annual Night Market where student organizations can gather to sell food from their respective cultural backgrounds.

  • One Night in Beijing

    Sat April 23, 2016

    UC Rangos

    Come and enjoy an evening of Chinese cultural performances, including dancing, singing, music, and a fashion show featuring both traditional and contemporary Chinese clothing. Experience Chinese culture and tradition while enjoying tasty snacks sold by various CMU Asian organizations.

  • Tangyuan Night

    Fall 2015

    Stever Kitchen

    Come and learn all about who we are and what we do. We will have a (free) hands-on tangyuan (rice balls) making session. We'll introduce ourselves and go over our upcoming events, but just come and chat with us.. we'd love to get to know you :) All people are welcome! All those new freshman looking for a fun and involved group on campus are especially encouraged to come hang out.

  • Mid-Autumn Festival

    Fall 2015

    UC Black Chairs

    Mid-Autumn Festival is the second-most celebrated Chinese holiday and dates back over 3,000 years. On this traditional holiday, families and friends get together to eat moon cakes and enjoy the brightness of the full moon ('shang yue'). For the Chinese, the full moon has always represented the gathering of friends and family, thus it is a time for reunions. ARCC's Mid-Autumn Festival consists of a fun-filled night of celebration, showcasing various dance and musical performances and, of course, moon cakes!

  • Dumpling Night

    Fall 2015

    Stever Kitchen

    Dumpling Night gives everyone a perfect opportunity to learn how to make dumplings! Since 2004, ARCC hosted the event with New House to provide students a chance to make their own dumplings. Every year, ARCC provides all the ingredients; all you have to do is show up! We also cook your dumplings on the spot so you can enjoy them right after you make them.
